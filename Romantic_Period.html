Romantic

Nocturne Op. 9 No. 2 – Frédéric Chopin

"Composed between 1830 and 1832, this Nocturne is one of Chopin’s most beloved works. It captures the Romantic era’s passion for emotional depth, with a lyrical melody and expressive rubato, delivering a sense of longing and tenderness through solo piano."

Hungarian Dance No. 5 – Johannes Brahms

"Published in 1869, Brahms’ Hungarian Dance No. 5 is infused with the fiery spirit of Romani folk music. Its quick dynamic shifts, catchy rhythms, and bold contrasts made it an instant hit. It represents how Romantic composers drew on folk roots to express national identity."

Symphonie Fantastique – March to the Scaffold – Hector Berlioz

"Written in 1830, Berlioz's Symphonie Fantastique tells the story of an artist's opium-fueled dream. The fourth movement, March to the Scaffold, depicts the protagonist’s execution. It showcases dramatic storytelling through music — a hallmark of Romantic orchestration."
