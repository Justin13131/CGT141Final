Medieval

“Ave Generosa” – Hildegard von Bingen

“Ave Generosa” is a tribute to the Virgin Mary, composed by Hildegard in the 12th century. It features modal melodies and sacred mysticism, expressing divine femininity and religious devotion in Hildegard’s visionary voice.

“Sumer Is Icumen In” – Anonymous (13th Century)

This is the earliest known canon (round) in the English language, dated around 1260. It celebrates the arrival of summer and showcases polyphonic innovation long before the Renaissance. It’s playful, rustic, and reflective of Medieval secular song.

“Cantiga 166: Como poden per sas culpas” – Alfonso X, Cantigas de Santa Maria

Part of the Cantigas de Santa Maria, a 13th-century collection of songs praising the Virgin Mary, compiled under King Alfonso X of Castile. These are some of the most extensive and beautifully preserved examples of Medieval monophonic music.
