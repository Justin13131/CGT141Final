Baroque

Brandenburg Concerto No. 3 – J.S. Bach

"Composed by Johann Sebastian Bach around 1721, the Brandenburg Concertos were a set of six instrumental works dedicated to the Margrave of Brandenburg. Concerto No. 3 is renowned for its lively and complex counterpoint, showcasing the Baroque love for structure, energy, and ornamentation. Bach’s mastery of fugal writing and instrumental interplay is on full display here.

Dido’s Lament (from Dido and Aeneas) – Henry Purcell

"Dido and Aeneas, written around 1689 by English composer Henry Purcell, is one of the earliest English operas. Dido’s Lament ("When I am laid in earth") is the tragic aria sung by Queen Dido as she faces death. It's famous for its haunting descending bass line (a ground bass), a hallmark of Baroque emotional expression and musical form.

The Four Seasons – Antonio Vivaldi

"Composed in 1723, The Four Seasons is a group of four violin concertos by Italian composer Antonio Vivaldi. Each concerto represents a different season and is accompanied by a sonnet, possibly written by Vivaldi himself. The music vividly paints scenes like birdsong in spring or thunder in summer, making it one of the earliest examples of program music.
