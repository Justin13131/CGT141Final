Renaissance

“Ave Maria” – Josquin des Prez

Composed around 1485, Ave Maria... Virgo Serena is one of the most famous motets of the Renaissance. Josquin des Prez pioneered expressive polyphony, and this piece showcases smooth imitation between vocal lines, deep reverence, and textual clarity — hallmarks of Renaissance sacred music.

“Flow My Tears” – John Dowland

Published in 1600, Dowland’s Flow My Tears is a melancholic lute song that became one of the most iconic secular works of the late Renaissance. It reflects the era’s fascination with emotion, humanism, and personal expression, performed in English courts and salons.

“Il est bel et bon” – Pierre Passereau

This French chanson, composed around 1534, is a humorous and lively example of Renaissance secular vocal music. It features fast-paced rhythms and playful imitation between voices, typical of village scenes and popular music of the time.
